My passion carried me then to graduate with a Master 1 in Biology and Master 2 in Forensic Science at the same university. During this time, I have also developed an interest in programming, as I was impressed by its power to solve real-world problems using only logical patterns! Therefore, I chose to complete my master’s thesis in digital forensics (specifically forensic analysis of WhatsApp). Combined with my biology-related coursework, it created an amazing two-pillar master’s experience.

Today, I am very proud to have built a multidisciplinary profile. I have used several techniques in molecular biology, genetics, microbiology, and immunology along with wide theoretical knowledge in genomics, hematology, endocrinology, bacteriology, biostatistics, and medical and population genetics. Simultaneously, I used the coding experience I gained to build purpose-specific tools and scripts. In addition, my experience in a hospital medical laboratory expanded my knowledge in the medical biology field. I performed and analyzed serologic tests, routine hematology testing, and bacteriology tests.

My interest in biological field work continued after I became a private tutor. I keep my learning at the core of my teaching, as teaching demands broader knowledge in subject matter, specifically with college level courses. Now I feel that it is time to take the next step and pursue a Ph.D. degree. I believe it is a great opportunity for me to expand my knowledge and develop further my research skills.
